1.  The starting point of content creation is low. It originates from the germination of an idea—perhaps an inspiration, a piece of text, an image, or even a slogan. In its initial stage, this content is often fragmented and unformed, with low potential energy.
2.  As the content is gradually refined and enriched, its potential energy begins to rise. The author continuously revises, supplements, and optimizes the content, making it more complete and attractive. This stage may involve multiple iterations, with each improvement injecting more value into the content.
3.  When content needs to be published, it must undergo a dimensionality reduction projection to adapt to different platforms and channels. This process may lead to the simplification or omission of certain details, causing a dip in the content's potential energy.
4.  Audience feedback and interaction further influence the content's potential energy. If the content resonates and receives positive feedback, its potential energy rises again, forming a virtuous cycle.

This is precisely the meaning of 'N' in the name CZON: the N-shaped energy curve from content creation to distribution (N-shaped Content Creation and Distribution Energy Curve).
