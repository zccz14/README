## Core Philosophies (Using Life Examples)

### "Embrace the Finite, Design the Infinite": Accept Imperfection to Create Perfection

**Simply put**: Acknowledge AI's limitations, then use clever methods to work around them.

**An analogy**: Asking a child to buy soy sauce. They might not find the store, miscalculate change, or forget to buy it. But you wouldn't stop them from helpingâ€”you'd tell them where the store is, give enough money, and provide a checklist. AI is similar; it's not omnipotent, but with the right methods, it can deliver maximum value.

**How to do it**:

- **Don't make AI do too many things at once**: Like you wouldn't have one employee handle sales, finance, HR, and R&D simultaneously. AI excels at focused tasks, not being a jack-of-all-trades. Break big tasks into small ones, each AI doing just one thing.
- **Set a budget for AI**: AI thinking costs money (Token consumption), like paying employees. Set a budget for AI to work within; don't let it think indefinitely.
- **Allow AI to be imperfect**: AI's first output might not be great; that's okay. Design a "check-feedback-revise" loop, giving AI a chance to improve. Like a manager giving feedback after assigning a task, not expecting perfection on the first try.

### "Deep Creation, Shallow Distribution": Craft Content Meticulously, but Present It Simply

**Simply put**: Think deeply and polish during creation, but lower barriers and simplify for distribution.

**An analogy**: Like cooking a dish. In the kitchen, you can fry, stir, boil, etc.â€”it's complex. But when serving guests, you just present a delicious, well-presented plate; guests don't need to know the entire cooking process. Creation is "deep processing"; distribution is "shallow presentation."

**How to do it**:

- **Spend more time creating**: Good content needs polishing. Like CZ's INSIGHTS articles in this repositoryâ€”each is thoughtfully crafted, not hastily written.
- **Lower barriers during distribution**: If only experts can understand your writing, its reach is limited. Good creators should make their content both professional and accessible. Like a good teacher who explains complex knowledge simply.

### "Controllable Trust": Let Humans Confidently Delegate Tasks to AI

**Simply put**: Establish mechanisms so humans trust AI won't run amok and can intervene when needed.

**An analogy**: Like letting a new employee work independently, but not completely hands-off. You'd set workflows, conduct regular checks, and retain approval authority. The employee, knowing there's oversight, works more diligently; you, knowing there's a safety net, worry less.

**How to do it**:

- **Make AI explain what it's doing**: Don't let AI be a black box outputting results without explanation. Require AI to explain its reasoning process so humans can understand and review.
- **Retain human approval at key nodes**: Like major contracts needing a leader's signature, AI's final output needs human confirmation. This isn't distrust but responsibility.
- **Define AI's permission boundaries**: Clearly state what AI can and cannot do. Like setting limits for a new employee's authority to prevent overstepping.

### "Complexity is the Inevitable Path to Cognition": Don't Try to Take Shortcuts

**Simply put**: True understanding must go through complexity; shortcuts only keep you at the surface.

**An analogy**: Learning to swim. Reading books alone won't work. You must get in the water, swallow some, struggle a bit, to truly learn. Investing, programming, writing code are the sameâ€”there's a chasm between reading others' work and doing it yourself.

**How to do it**:
