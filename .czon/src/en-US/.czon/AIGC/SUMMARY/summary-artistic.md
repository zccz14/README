The interaction among the three is like the three-body problem in celestial mechanics – **a steady-state solution may not exist**, instead exhibiting limit cycles, quasi-periodicity, or chaotic behavior.

He constructs an exquisite profit-risk-cost matrix, showing perfect axial symmetry: changes in each market variable create winners, losers, and those consumed, with no variable being beneficial or harmful to all capital types. This is precisely the manifestation of three-body balance.

And the most applause-worthy insight lies in the phase analysis: **All three exits from the Hot state (three highs) lead to loss periods** – no matter which variable falls first, one type of capital suffers. Therefore, in an overheated state, any directional bet is gambling. Conversely, **all three exits from the Cold state (three lows) lead to gain periods** – no matter which variable rises first, one type of capital benefits. Therefore, in a cold state, the key is to stay in the game.
